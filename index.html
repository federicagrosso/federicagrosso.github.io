Welcome!

I am a PhD student at the CTGlab (CNCR), VU Amsterdam, under the supervision of Prof. Danielle Posthuma. My research focuses on developing and applying statistical methods to tackle heterogeneity in the genetics of complex traits, with a particular interest in neuropsychiatric phenotypes.

Previously, I completed my Master’s thesis at the Institute for Genetic and Biomedical Research (IRGB-CNR), where I explored statistical genetics and causal inference, investigating the causal relationships between the gut microbiome and age-related traits through Mendelian randomization, under the supervision of Dr. Serena Sanna and Dr. Daniela Zanetti.

I enjoy the rigor of mathematics and the elegance of formal models, and I aim to apply this precision to uncover insights in genetics and complex biological systems.

If you want to chat or learn more about my work, feel free to contact me via email!
